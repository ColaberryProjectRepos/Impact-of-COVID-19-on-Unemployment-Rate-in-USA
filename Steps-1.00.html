Project Overview 1

Impact of COVID-19 on Unemployment in US


Technologies: Python, Jupyter Notebook, R

Data Sources: Covid Tracking Project, US Bureau of Labor Statistics, US Department of Labor Employment and Training Administration

Description     : We tracked the spread of COVID in US and analyzed top 10 states in total cases and their death count. The result showed that even though California, Florida and Texas has more number of cases, Northeast states has the highest fatality rate for corona virus in the nation.

As states work to slow the spread of COVID, thousands of workers are finding themselves unemployed leading to a surge in application for unemployment benefits. Looking at the current Unemployment Rate trend we can see that Unemployment rose higher in three months of COVID-19 than it did in two years of the Great Recession. The rise in the number of unemployed workers due to COVID-19 is substantially greater than the increase due to the Great Recession. The Great Recession, which officially lasted from December 2007 to June 2009, pushed the unemployment rate to a peak of 10.6% in January 2010, considerably less than the rate currently. Unemployment among all groups of workers increased sharply in the COVID-19 recession. But the experiences of several groups of workers in the COVID-19 outbreak vary notably from how they experienced the Great Recession.
We compared Unemployment rate from April, May and June with the highest ever rate recorded and observed that April 2020 is the highest ever rate for most of the states.

The impact of COVID and increase in Unemployment rate resulted a spike in Unemployment Insurance Initial claims. Initial claims are an employment report that measures the number of new jobless claims filed by individuals seeking to receive unemployment benefits. The claims stagnated between 1.4-to 1.5 million a week during the last month, remains at more than double the pre-pandemic record, pointing to the magnitude of the crisis.
